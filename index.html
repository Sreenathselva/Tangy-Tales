About Oranges
Oranges, the vibrant and juicy citrus fruits, have long been celebrated for their refreshing flavor and numerous health benefits. Packed with vitamin C, oranges are not only delicious but also contribute to a strengthened immune system and radiant skin. These sunny fruits come in various varieties, each with its own unique taste profile, ranging from the sweet and tangy Valencia to the aromatic and rich blood orange.
Types of Oranges to try
Naval Oranges
Naval oranges are a popular type of sweet and seedless citrus fruit known for their juicy and flavorful taste. They get their name from a small, navel-like formation on one end. These oranges have a bright orange color, are easy to peel, and are loved for their balanced sweetness and lower acidity. Packed with vitamin C, naval oranges contribute to a healthy immune system. Whether eaten fresh, juiced, or used in cooking, naval oranges are a favorite choice for those who enjoy a refreshing and tasty citrus experience.
Cara Cara Oranges
The cara cara orange is a type of navel orange and also seedless and sweet. It has pink to red flesh due to its lycopene pigmentation (a type of carotenoid). The body converts some carotenoids into vitamin A, an essential nutrient for immune and eye health and normal growth and development. The juice of cara cara orange doesn't turn bitter upon exposure to air, unlike regular navel orange. This makes it a versatile fruit in a variety of recipes, including salads, sauces, orange juice, and baked goods.
Valencia Oranges
Valencia oranges are sweet and extremely juicy, making them a perfect fruit for juices and smoothies. They have a slightly pebbly rind and occasional seeds. These oranges are in season from February through early November, with peak availability from July through October. Valencia oranges are great for summer grilling and pair well with savory and spicy foods like pork dishes and salsa. In addition to nutrients like vitamin C and potassium, oranges also provide folate, an important B vitamin required for making DNA and red blood cells. Folate is also essential during pregnancy to prevent neural tube defects.
Mandarin Orange
Mandarin oranges are a type of sweet orange that's in season from November through April. They are small, have a medium to thick loosely adherent rind, and a relatively smooth surface. If not harvested at peak internal ripeness, mandarin oranges deteriorate in quality and their rinds become puffy. Besides packing fresh or packaged mandarin oranges in a lunch box, try adding slices to Greek yogurt or oatmeal for a refreshing and nutritious breakfast.
Clementines
Known for their small size and easy-to-peel bright orange skin, clementines are a type of mandarin orange. These seedless oranges are the smallest type of mandarin orange, making them a perfect snack for kids. Clementines are typically sold in 2 to 5-pound (lbs.) bags in the grocery store. Packing a mighty punch of vitamin C, a single clementine delivers 36 mg, an impressive 40% of the Daily Value, all within its petite form. Pair clementines with cottage cheese or nuts for a balanced snack.
Tangerines
Tangerines, a delightful variety of mandarin oranges, are cherished for their vibrant flavor and easy-to-peel nature. These small citrus fruits are characterized by their bright orange hue and a slightly flattened shape. What sets tangerines apart is their remarkable sweetness and distinctive aroma. The peel is thin and peels away effortlessly, revealing juicy, segmented flesh inside. Tangerines offer a burst of citrusy goodness, combining a perfect balance of sweetness and tanginess. Rich in vitamin C and other nutrients, they contribute to a healthy diet and are a popular choice for snacking, salads, and fresh juice.
Sumo Oranges
Sumo oranges are a special type of citrus fruit known for their exceptional taste and unique appearance. They are a cross between a mandarin and a California navel orange, resulting in large, bumpy-skinned fruits with a distinctive top knot, resembling a sumo wrestler's hairstyle. Despite their unusual appearance, Sumo oranges are easy to peel and have a sweet, juicy, and seedless interior with a well-balanced flavor of sweetness and a hint of tartness. They are a favorite among citrus enthusiasts and are eagerly anticipated during their limited winter season. The combination of size, flavor, and convenience has made Sumo oranges a sought-after and beloved addition to the citrus market.
Blood Oranges
Blood oranges are a captivating citrus variety known for their unique appearance and distinct flavor. The deep red to maroon hue of their flesh sets them apart from traditional oranges, owing to the presence of anthocyanin pigments. These pigments not only create a visually striking fruit but also contribute to a subtly different taste, often described as slightly berry-like or with hints of raspberry. Beyond their appearance, blood oranges share the same refreshing and tangy citrus profile as regular oranges, making them versatile for both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
Seville Oranges
Seville oranges, known for their distinctive bitter taste, are a citrus variety with a unique role in culinary applications. These oranges are prized for their high pectin content, making them a preferred choice for making marmalade. Their vibrant, orange skin encases a tart and slightly sour flesh, which, when combined with sugar, transforms into a flavorful and zesty spread. Seville oranges are not typically consumed fresh due to their strong bitterness but are instead celebrated for the rich, robust flavor they lend to jams and preserves. Beyond their culinary applications, the aromatic zest of Seville oranges also finds its way into various recipes, adding a citrusy kick to both sweet and savory dishes. In the world of citrus, Seville oranges stand out for their unique contribution to the culinary arts, making them a key ingredient in the creation of the beloved Seville orange marmalade.
